About me

I was born in Northeast China, right on the border between China and North Korea. For some reason, I found myself zig-zagging in a easterly direction, finishing my high school in Northeast China, and across the Pacific, I got my B.S. from Cal State, East Bay in California. I migrated on to graduate school at Utah, completing a Ph.D. with Scott L. Anserson in 2016, then I joined Sylvia T. Ceyer's lab as a postdoc at MIT in 2017.

Apart from my research in the field of surface chemistry, I also enjoy brewing beer, and learning some cool stuff about Python, or computer programming in general. I have to say, it is a good time now to be a computer programmer.
